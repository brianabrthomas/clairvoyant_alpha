MacroEconomic Data

800,000+ time series from FRED, IMF, BLS, BEA, World Bank, and EIA. HMM/GMM regime clustering produces parsimonious macro states with Markov transition probabilities. Z-score event detection identifies statistically abnormal market responses demanding explanation.

Regime Classification

Growth Expansion
Rising GDP, low unemployment, earnings growth. Equities reward momentum, options vol compressed.
Disinflation
Falling inflation, rate cut expectations, duration assets bid. Growth factors dominate.
Policy Tightening
Rising rates, hawkish policy, multiple compression. Balance sheet quality differentiates.
Liquidity Crisis
Correlation spikes, credit spreads widen, funding stress. Defensive positioning, tail hedging.

Capabilities

HMM / GMM Regime Clustering
Unsupervised identification of latent macro states from normalized indicator vectors. BIC-optimized cluster count with soft membership probabilities.
Transition Matrices
Markov chain state-to-state transition probabilities with historical calibration. Fan charts for regime evolution over 3/6/12 month horizons.
Z-Score Event Detection
Multi-tier thresholding (1.5σ unusual, 2.0σ likely catalyst, 3.0σ+ structural shock) on rolling-window normalized indicators.
Indicator Heat Maps
Visual representation of economic indicator states across categories: growth, labor, inflation, credit, housing, trade. Color-coded by z-score magnitude.
Vintage-Aware Retrieval
ALFRED-style "as-of" data access. Every query returns what was known when — preventing look-ahead bias in backtesting and regime assignment.
Cross-Indicator Correlation
Distance correlation, tail dependence, and copula fits between indicators. Significance scoring to identify statistically potent co-movements.

Per-asset sensitivity to each macro regime. Rolling + regime-conditional betas stored as statistical artifacts with confidence intervals.
