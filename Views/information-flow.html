Information Flow

Research reports, earnings surprises, and analyst revisions form the informational backbone of price discovery. High β to research signals institutional ownership and sell-side credibility. Information is ranked by an evidence hierarchy — not by volume or recency.

Revision Momentum Signal Srevision,t = Σj wj · ΔEPSj,t / σhistorical

Evidence Hierarchy (CFA-Grade)

Tier 1 Earnings results, guidance changes, regulatory filings, macro policy announcements, management statements
Tier 2 Sell-side revisions, credit rating changes, official data releases, industry association reports
Tier 3 Media narratives, analyst commentary, market speculation, expert network transcripts

Capabilities

Earnings Surprise Measurement
Standardized Unexpected Earnings (SUE) computation against consensus. Decomposition into revenue vs. margin vs. below-the-line components.
Analyst Revision Intensity
Breadth, magnitude, and direction of consensus revisions tracked per entity. Rolling revision momentum scored as a z-score signal.
Guidance Change Detection
Automated extraction and classification of forward guidance from earnings calls and filings. Magnitude scoring relative to prior consensus range.
Research Report Parsing
Structured extraction of price targets, ratings, key assumptions, and risk factors from sell-side research. Source-weighted credibility scoring.
Cross-Entity Information Spillover
How does information from one company's report transmit to peers? Lead-lag, magnitude, and regime-conditional spillover coefficients.
Post-Earnings Drift Analysis
Tracking abnormal returns after earnings for drift persistence, decay rates, and regime-conditional behavior. Saturated vs. exploitable drift identification.
